What Is GEO? Get ChatGPT and Perplexity to Recommend Your Brand

Travis Chen 2026-05-06 8 min read

Have you noticed a trend? More and more people aren't "searching" anymore — they're "asking."

In the past, if you wanted to find "the best ramen in town," you'd search on Google and browse the results page. Now? You open ChatGPT and ask "What are the best ramen restaurants near me?" The AI gives you a curated list with recommendations and reasons.

Here's the question: Is your brand on that AI-recommended list?

This is exactly what GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is designed to address.

1. From SEO to GEO: Search Behavior Is Changing

Traditional SEO follows a simple logic: users type keywords into Google, Google shows ranked results, users click links to visit your website. Throughout this process, your goal is to rank higher.

But AI search works on completely different logic: users ask a question, the AI synthesizes information from multiple sources, and directly generates an answer. Users may never click into any website, but the AI will cite and recommend certain brands.

These AI search engines include:

  • ChatGPT — Equipped with search capabilities, citing web sources
  • Perplexity — A platform dedicated to AI search, with over 100 million monthly active users
  • Google AI Overview — AI summaries at the top of Google search results
  • Microsoft Copilot — AI assistant integrated into Bing search and Windows

2. Core GEO Strategies

How AI engines decide which content to cite differs from traditional SEO ranking factors. Here are the core GEO strategies:

1. Structured Data

AI relies on structured information even more than humans do. JSON-LD Schema is the best way to help AI understand your content:

  • FAQPage Schema — Mark up frequently asked questions in structured format; AI loves citing this type of content
  • Article Schema — Clearly mark the author, publication date, and topic category of articles
  • Organization Schema — Let AI know your brand name, service scope, and location
  • Product/Service Schema — Mark up your product and service information

2. Q&A-Format Content

AI search is fundamentally about "asking questions." If your website content is written in a Q&A format, AI can more easily extract and cite it:

  • Use H2/H3 headings as questions (e.g., "What size company is a CRM system suitable for?")
  • Provide clear, concise answers directly below each question
  • Naturally mention your brand name within the answers

3. Brand Mentions Strategy

AI models draw their knowledge from vast amounts of web data. How often and in what context your brand is mentioned online directly affects whether AI will recommend you.

  • Publish brand-related content on authoritative media outlets
  • Build brand presence in industry forums and communities
  • Ensure consistency of your brand name across all platforms

4. Authority and Credibility

AI tends to cite sources considered "authoritative." Ways to build authority include:

  • Articles with clear author information and professional background
  • Citing reliable data sources
  • Consistently producing high-quality expert content

3. GEO and SEO Don't Conflict

The good news is that GEO and SEO aren't mutually exclusive. In fact, doing GEO well typically also benefits SEO:

  • Structured data is already a ranking boost for Google SEO
  • Q&A-format content fits Google's Featured Snippet format
  • High-quality expert content is valued by every search engine

The difference is that traditional SEO heavily values backlinks, which have less impact on GEO, while GEO places more emphasis on content extractability and brand recognition.
